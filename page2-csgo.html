Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO)

Where Strategy and Precision Collide

First released in 2012, CS:GO remains a cornerstone of competitive esports. As a tactical first-person shooter (FPS), it pits two teams—Terrorists and Counter-Terrorists—against each other in objective-based matches.

Core Gameplay

Matches revolve around planting/defusing bombs (Bomb scenarios) or rescuing hostages. Players earn in-game currency to buy weapons, armor, and utilities (like grenades) each round, rewarding smart economy management.

Esports Legacy

Home to major tournaments like the Intel Extreme Masters (IEM) and PGL Major, with prize pools exceeding $2 million. Teams like NAVI and FaZe Clan have become global icons in the CS:GO scene.

Accessibility

Free-to-play on Steam, with optional cosmetic skins for weapons. Supports cross-platform play between PC and console, and offers a casual mode for new players to learn the ropes.
